The Challenge: Turning Waste into Wattage

The UAE produces over 3.27 million tonnes of food waste every year, leading to an eye-watering $3.5 billion in losses. But the true cost goes far beyond economics. When this organic waste ends up in landfills, it decomposes without oxygen—releasing methane, a greenhouse gas 25 times more potent than CO₂, and pushing our planet closer to climate tipping points.

Despite the nation's bold smart city ambitions and global sustainability commitments, real-time, IoT-powered bioconversion solutions like Microbial Fuel Cells (MFCs) remain dramatically underutilized. This represents a missed opportunity—not just to manage waste, but to transform it into clean, renewable energy.

At PowerSprout, we’re stepping into this gap—harnessing cutting-edge microbial tech to turn yesterday’s leftovers into tomorrow’s electricity.

Our Vision: Waste is Power

At PowerSprout, we’re reimagining food waste—not as trash, but as a powerful, untapped renewable energy source.

We envision a world where yesterday’s scraps become tomorrow’s electricity—clean, storable, and sensor-driven—through smart Microbial Fuel Cells, seamlessly monitored by scalable IoT systems.

Our mission begins in the UAE, with a bold goal: to power campuses, communities, and eventually entire cities using food waste. By converting organic leftovers into sustainable energy, we aim to lead the charge toward a carbon-conscious future, one waste-to-watt system at a time.
